We always took pictures to show her the next day and afterwards we would take off her glasses and put her to bed.

In 2009, fate brought Sarah and me together as roommates at Oral Roberts University. Some of my fondest memories with Sarah include going to various concerts. Sarah’s good-natured & motivating personality isn’t reserved just for concerts, I have witnessed Sarah take a compassionate approach to everyone she encounters. Whether family, friend, foe, or a complete stranger- Sarah is always ready to lift a helping hand & speak words of encouragement to everyone she meets. So, whether we were simply meeting for local burgers in OKC, taking a vacation trip to another city, or just having a brief phone conversation- anytime I get to hangout with Sarah I know I’ll leave our encounter in a happier mindset because Sarah’s joyfulness is contagious.

Over the past almost 10 years, we have worked to fostered a faith-based genuine friendship that’s so strong we can’t just call each other “friends”, but rather “soul-sisters” with a lifelong unbreakable bond. That’s why I know she’ll make a phenomenal wife (and one day a mother too) and I am over-joyed to to celebrate in her union to become Mrs. Ofodire. And I look forward expectantly to see where God will lead & bless Sarah within the next decade & beyond in our friendship.

My name is Russell Oyewole and I met Dr. Chisom Ofodire (unofficially) for the first time in the winter of 2006. We eventfully crossed paths in the parking of a Target store where I was working as a cart attendant; the rest is history.

Dr. Chisom is a brother, brother in Christ, pal, buddy, friend, monopoly game partner, academic motivator, bandmate, personal physicians (pro bono) and inside jokes comrade. It is a difficult task to write in brevity about Chisom because of how many memories we’ve established over the years. I remember several times we’ve “played surgeon”; we would reenact a surgery scene from washing our hands, putting on gloves, walking into the imaginary theatre backwards, and calling out surgical procedures. I recall several mock debates we’ve had; political debates, debates about politics, presidential political debates, and debates about everything else. I recount the several menial jobs we’ve undertaken; tossing newspapers, being lifeguards, 5linx (my income). I can’t the forget the countless hours of studying at the infamous JEFJEN which as culminated to the title of Doctor. I’m grateful for the opportunities we were given to know and receive Jesus as Lord and savior; this crucial relationship set the foundation and course for our lives.

We’ve spoken several times, even joked of the notion of marriage; who will get married first, what kind of gal will we marry etc and I’m ecstatic the moment is here. Thank you for choosing me to be a groomsman.

Congratulation my brother on your marriage, I hope to continue to be a part of your new family (you and lovely Chidinma). Praying that your union will begin with God and be sustained by God until the End.
